Redbox Has Ceased Operations

As of 2024, Redbox has permanently shut down all rental operations. Most kiosks have been removed from stores and decommissioned. If you still have a rental disc, you can keep it as there's no way to process returns and their servers are offline.

For Customers

Looking for information about rentals, returns, or your account? Here's what you need to know about Redbox's operations.

  • • All kiosks are permanently offline and cannot process payments, nor access your Redbox Perks account
  • • Customer service and phone support is no longer available
  • • If you still have a disc rental, keep it - returns can't be processed
  • • Nearly all kiosks have been decommissioned and removed from stores

Timeline

August 2022

Redbox is acquired by Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment

In August 2022, Redbox was acquired by Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment for $375 million, a change in ownership that would lead to the company's eventual downfall.

End of 2023

Huge losses reported by CSSE, creating concerns about funding

During 2023, Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment reported roughly $636 million in net losses, raising concerns about the company's financial stability.

April 2024

Continued financial struggles for CSSE, debt begins to worsen

Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment reports nearly $53 million in losses during the first quarter of 2024, with their debt likely exceeding the value of their assets.

May 2024

Redbox employee credit cards begin to decline, forcing employees to stay home

In May 2024, reports from employees revealed how Redbox employee credit cards (used for expenses such as gas) were being declined, forcing field service employees to stay home for an entire week.

June 2024

An SEC filing reveals board of directors had been dissolved

On June 11th, 2024, an SEC filing revealed that Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment's board of directors had been dissolved, with chairman and CEO Bill Rouhana Jr. holding 79% of the voting power.

June 2024

Redbox fails to make payroll on time, employees report missed payments

In mid-June 2024, Redbox was unable to meet payroll, leaving employees without paychecks. CEO Bill Rouhana assured staff that payment would arrive five days late, but multiple delays followed, signaling a loss of funding within the company.

June 2024

Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy

On June 28th, 2024, Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy after failing to pay employees and vendors for over 4 weeks. CSSE listed debts of $970 million and consolidated assets of $414 million as of March 31st, 2024.

July 2024

Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment converts to Chapter 7 bankruptcy

The following month of the initial bankruptcy filing, Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment started the conversion to Chapter 7 bankruptcy, involving the liquidation of its assets. All Redbox services, including its streaming and mobile app, were shut down.

April 2025

Foreclosure sale announced for Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment and its assets

A court filing during early April announced a foreclosure sale of Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment and its subsidiaries, including Redbox, scheduled for April 23, 2025 in New York, NY.

October 2025

Grove Street Partners offers $100M for Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment IP assets

In October 2025, Grove Street Partners submitted a $100 million offer to purchase Chicken Soup for the Soul Entertainment's intellectual property litigation assets, according to a court filing.


Sources: Wikipedia, Hollywood Reporter, Variety, The Verge [1], United States Bankruptcy Court, The Verge [2]
